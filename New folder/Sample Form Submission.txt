Automation Description *
Briefly describe: What problem does this automation solve? How does it work? What makes it valuable for your D2C company?
Problem solved: D2C companies and service-based businesses waste 2-4 hours per client manually drafting proposals, calculating invoices, sending emails, following up, and logging CRM entries. This repetitive cycle creates bottlenecks, inconsistent branding, delayed responses, lost deals, and zero visibility into the sales pipeline. How it works: A single form submission triggers a 4-workflow, 58-node autonomous pipeline. The intake form validates input, calculates GST, generates unique IDs, and fires the data via webhook. GPT-4o writes a personalised, 8-section proposal tailored to the client's industry, service needs, and budget. Two parallel branches simultaneously generate a branded proposal PDF (via Google Slides API) and a GST-calculated invoice PDF (via Google Sheets API). Both are emailed to the client with professional HTML formatting and interactive Accept/Decline buttons within 30 seconds. The client is auto-logged into a Google Sheets CRM with 17 tracked columns. A separate Follow-Up Scheduler workflow runs on a timer, reads the CRM, and fires staged follow-up emails - Day 3, Day 7, and Day 14 - each with Accept/Decline buttons. If the client clicks Accept or Decline at any point, the CRM updates instantly, a welcome/acknowledgement email fires, and the follow-up sequence stops. Real-time Telegram notifications keep the sales team informed at every stage. What makes it valuable for a D2C company: Speed-to-client drops from hours to 30 seconds - the fastest proposal wins the deal 100% brand consistency - every proposal follows the same professional template Zero revenue leakage - automated follow-ups ensure no lead is forgotten Full pipeline visibility - the CRM sheet gives founders a real-time view of pipeline status Near-zero marginal cost - after setup, each proposal costs ˜?1.50 (OpenAI API), making it viable for high volumes Scalable without hiring - one person can handle the proposal volume that would normally require a 3-person sales ops team.

My build is better than what was taught for six specific reasons: 1. Multi-Step Intelligent Follow-Up Cadence (not just one follow-up) The taught version sends one follow-up after 3 days. My enhanced build implements a 3-stage escalation sequence using a dedicated scheduler workflow that runs on a timer and reads the CRM Google Sheet to determine which stage each proposal is at. It sends a Day 3 reminder, a Day 7 urgency email, and a Day 14 closing notice. Each stage checks CRM status before sending - if the client accepted or declined at any point, the sequence stops automatically. 2. One-Click Accept/Reject Webhooks in the Email I added two webhook-triggered URLs embedded as styled HTML buttons in the proposal email: " Accept Proposal" and " Decline." These are handled by a dedicated webhook workflow. When the client clicks Accept, the webhook reads the CRM, updates the status to "Proposal Accepted", sends a professional "Welcome Aboard" email with next-steps messaging, and pushes a Telegram notification. Both paths respond with styled HTML confirmation pages, turning a static email into an interactive decision tool. 3. Real-Time Telegram Notifications for the Sales Team Every key event pushes a formatted Markdown notification to a Telegram bot via direct API calls: Proposal Sent, Follow-Up Sent, Proposal Closed, Proposal Accepted, and Proposal Declined. Each notification includes client name, company, email, service type, deal value, and proposal ID. This eliminates the need to constantly check the CRM sheet and creates a real-time command centre feel. 4. AI-Generated Competitive Positioning Section My enhancement adds a dedicated section - "Competitive Positioning" - where GPT-4o analyses the client's industry and generates strategic positioning text about how the proposed service places the client ahead of competitors. The result is injected into the Google Slides template via the {{competitive_positioning}} placeholder. This transforms the proposal into a consultative pitch. 5. Revenue Pipeline Dashboard (Auto-Updated) The workflow automatically feeds a "Dashboard" tab in the CRM Google Sheet via a dedicated "Dashboard Updater" node. Every proposal sent appends a row with date, proposal ID, client name, company, total value, and service type. Combined with spreadsheet formulas, this provides D2C founders with an instant view of total pipeline value, proposals by status, and deal metrics - without any BI tool. 6. Multi-Language Proposal Support The intake form includes an optional "Preferred Language" field (English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada).
